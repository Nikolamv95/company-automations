# Desire Stack Model — Reference

Eugene Schwartz's framework for mapping and scoring mass desires. Used in Steps 5 and 6.

---

## The 4-Layer Desire Stack

| Layer | What It Is | Example (weight loss) |
|-------|-----------|----------------------|
| **Surface Desire** | What they say they want (socially acceptable) | "I want to lose weight" |
| **Real Desire** | What they actually want (specific transformation) | "I want to fit into my old jeans and feel attractive to my husband again" |
| **Suppressed Desire** | What they want but won't admit (most powerful fuel) | "I want to be the thinnest person in the room and feel superior" |
| **Identity Desire** | Who they want to become | "I want to be the woman who is effortlessly fit and disciplined" |

**The suppressed desire is often the strongest motivator.** Copy rarely states it directly — it activates it.

---

## Desire Scoring — Schwartz 3 Dimensions

For each desire, score on:

| Dimension | Definition | Score | Description |
|-----------|-----------|-------|-------------|
| **Urgency** | How immediately do they need this solved? | 1-5 | 1 = eventually / 5 = right now, consuming them |
| **Staying Power** | Does this desire repeat or fade? | 1-5 | 1 = temporary / 5 = chronic, daily |
| **Scope** | How many people share this desire? | 1-5 | 1 = niche few / 5 = mass market |

**Total score = Urgency + Staying Power + Scope (max 15)**

Higher total score = more scalable desire for advertising.

---

## Fear Stack (for each top desire)

Every desire has a corresponding fear. Map both — copy can lead with either.

| Fear Type | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Fear of NOT achieving** | "What if I never lose this weight?" |
| **Fear of trying and failing again** | "I've tried everything — what if this doesn't work either?" |
| **Social fear** | Judgment, embarrassment, loss of status or attractiveness |
| **Identity fear** | "What if I'm just the kind of person who can't do this?" |

---

## Desire Inventory Template (per desire)

1. **Desire Statement** — exact customer language (from forums, reviews, social)
2. **Intensity Score** — 1 (mild preference) to 10 (consuming obsession)
3. **Purchase Proximity** — High / Medium / Low (how directly does satisfying this require buying?)
4. **Fear Counterpart** — the fear sitting on the other side of this desire
5. **Best Trigger** — one-sentence hook that activates this desire

---

## Step 6: Desire Validation Criteria

When validating desires against market evidence, check:

| Test | Pass Condition |
|------|---------------|
| **Market evidence** | Real quotes from forums, reviews, YouTube comments exist — NOT hypothetical |
| **Competitive messaging** | Competitors are already targeting this desire (proves market) |
| **Emotional resonance** | Reading the desire statement creates a physical reaction (nodding, discomfort) |
| **Specificity** | Can anchor a specific, testable claim (not vague) |
| **Advertising scalability** | Can be dramatized visually and emotionally in an ad |

---

## Marketing Angle Types (Step 9)

| Angle Type | Lead With | Best Awareness Level |
|------------|-----------|---------------------|
| **Problem** | Amplify the pain — cost of inaction | Level 1-2 |
| **Mechanism** | Unique solution — why this works when others don't | Level 2-3 |
| **Proof** | Results — let the transformation sell itself | Level 3-4 |
| **Identity** | Who they become — new self-image | Level 4-5 |
| **Enemy** | Name the real cause — product is the fix | Level 2-3 |
| **Curiosity** | Hide the mechanism — tease the result | Level 1-3 |
| **Contrarian** | Challenge conventional wisdom | Level 2-4 |

---

## Evidence Standards

All desires must be grounded in real market evidence:
- Reddit threads (include subreddit and approximate date)
- Amazon reviews (include product category)
- Forum posts (include platform)
- YouTube comment sections
- Facebook group posts

**Never use:** "People probably want..." or "It's likely that..." — find actual evidence.

**Format for quotes:** "verbatim quote here" — [Platform, ~Month Year]
