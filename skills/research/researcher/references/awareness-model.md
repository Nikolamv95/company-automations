# Schwartz Awareness Model — Reference

Eugene Schwartz's 5-stage awareness model. Use to map where the target audience is, and what copy must accomplish at each level.

---

## The 5 Stages

| Level | State | What They Know | What Copy Must Do |
|-------|-------|---------------|-------------------|
| 1 — Unaware | Don't know they have a problem | Nothing — problem doesn't exist in their mind | Dramatize the problem. Don't lead with solution. |
| 2 — Problem Aware | Know the problem, don't know solutions exist | "I have this problem but I don't know what to do" | Lead with the problem. Introduce that a solution category exists. |
| 3 — Solution Aware | Know solutions exist, don't know yours | "I've heard of X type of solutions" | Lead with mechanism — why yours is different from what they've tried. |
| 4 — Product Aware | Know your product, haven't bought | "I've heard of [product] but haven't pulled the trigger" | Overcome objections. Strengthen the offer. Remove risk. |
| 5 — Most Aware | Ready to buy | Knows everything, just needs the right offer | Make the offer and close fast. |

---

## Copywriting Implications Per Level

### Level 1 — Unaware
- Open with a story or observation that reveals the problem
- Never mention the solution in the headline
- Build problem awareness before introducing any category
- Emotional hook: dramatize the consequence they're already experiencing but haven't named

### Level 2 — Problem Aware
- Open with the problem stated in the avatar's exact language
- Validate their frustration first
- Then introduce: "here's what's actually causing this..."
- Curiosity hook works well: tease the mechanism without naming it

### Level 3 — Solution Aware
- They've tried things before — acknowledge that
- Lead with WHY other solutions fail (the UMP — Unique Mechanism of Problem)
- Then reveal what makes yours different (the UMS — Unique Mechanism of Solution)
- Curiosity/proof hooks work: "the X that actually works"

### Level 4 — Product Aware
- They know the product — don't re-introduce it from scratch
- Lead with the strongest proof (testimonial, specific result)
- Address the #1 objection directly
- Make the offer irresistible (guarantee, bonus, urgency)

### Level 5 — Most Aware
- Skip the story — they know it
- Lead directly with the offer
- Urgency and specificity close: "X% off, ends Sunday"
- Identity hooks: speak to who they're becoming

---

## Behavioral Evidence to Look For

When determining awareness level for a product, look for:

- **Level 1-2**: Reddit posts saying "why do I feel X?" with no solution searches
- **Level 2-3**: Forum threads asking "what's the best [solution category] for X?"
- **Level 3-4**: Product comparison searches, "vs" queries, review-seeking behavior
- **Level 4-5**: Brand name searches, "discount for X", "does X work" searches

---

## Key Principle

**Awareness level determines the headline.** Getting this wrong means:
- Too advanced: audience doesn't understand what you're talking about
- Too basic: audience feels talked down to and disengages

Always confirm awareness level from research before writing any copy.
