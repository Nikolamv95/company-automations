# Hook Patterns — Formula Bank

Formula templates by hook type and platform. Fill in the brackets for the current product/avatar.

---

## By Hook Type

### Curiosity
```
"The [counterintuitive thing] that [unexpected positive result]"
"Why [common belief everyone holds] is [surprisingly wrong]"
"What [trusted authority] never tells you about [topic avatar cares about]"
"The [weird/odd] [thing] that [desirable result]"
```

### Fear / Pain
```
"If you [common behavior the avatar does], you might be [negative consequence they don't expect]"
"Every day you [don't do X], [cost/risk] gets [progressively worse]"
"Warning: [action avatar is taking] could be [causing the problem they're trying to fix]"
"The [hidden thing] that's [silently hurting you / your X]"
```

### Desire / Outcome
```
"[Specific result] in [specific timeframe] — without [common sacrifice]"
"How [specific person like avatar] [got exact result] [in short time]"
"Discover how to [primary desire] — even if [most common objection]"
```

### Identity / Pattern Interrupt
```
"For [specific avatar identity] who are ready to [transformation]"
"The [identity the avatar wants] who finally [specific result]"
"[Unexpected statement that creates cognitive dissonance about their current approach]"
```

---

## By Platform

### Facebook / Meta
- Emotion-first. Fear and desire hooks outperform curiosity.
- Visual hooks work: "Before you scroll past this..."
- Personal story openers perform well: "I was [relatable situation] when..."

### YouTube (first 5 seconds)
- Spoken, non-skippable — rhythm matters
- 3-beat opening: "I lost. Twenty-three. Pounds."
- Start mid-sentence or with a bold claim
- No warm-up — cut straight to the hook

### Native (Taboola / Outbrain)
- Curiosity dominates
- Tabloid style: "The [weird thing] that [unexpected outcome]"
- Numbers and specifics: "Doctors baffled by 67-year-old's result"

### TikTok
- Pattern interrupt first
- Start with action, controversy, or counterintuitive statement
- Hooks are often visual + spoken together — write for both

## Awareness Level → Best Hook Type

| Level | State | Best Hook Type |
|-------|-------|---------------|
| 1-2 | Unaware / Problem Aware | Fear/Pain — dramatize the problem |
| 3 | Solution Aware | Curiosity — tease the mechanism |
| 4 | Product Aware | Desire/Proof — show the transformation |
| 5 | Most Aware | Direct / Identity — speak to who they're becoming |
