# Video Ad Format Templates

Full script templates for each platform and length. All scripts assume DR/DTC health or supplement brand context.

---

## Facebook 30s — Cold Traffic Prospecting

Primary format for Facebook cold audiences. Must work muted (text overlay) and with sound.

```
[TEXT OVERLAY: Bold hook claim]
[VISUAL: Pattern interrupt — person, before/after, or strong visual]

[0-3s — HOOK — spoken]
"Stop doing [common behavior] if you want [desire]."

[3-10s — PROBLEM]
[CUT TO: Avatar-relatable scene]
"Most women over 45 who can't lose belly fat aren't doing anything wrong."

[PAUSE]

"Their body is."

[TEXT OVERLAY: "It's not willpower — it's cortisol"]

[10-20s — MECHANISM TEASE]
"After menopause, cortisol levels spike."

"And when cortisol is high — your body stores fat."

"No matter what you eat."

[TEXT OVERLAY: Key claim or stat]

[20-28s — PROOF TEASE]
"Over 4,000 women have used this discovery to lose [result] in [timeframe]."

[VISUAL: Real customer photo / before-after]

[28-30s — CTA]
"Tap below to see how."

[TEXT OVERLAY: "See how it works →"]
```

## Long-Form VSL — 10-Minute Script Outline

Used on landing pages for cold or warm traffic. Full RMBC structure.

```
[HOOK — 0-30s]
Three hook variants (pick one to test):
  A) Result: "What if losing belly fat after 45 had nothing to do with diet or exercise?"
  B) Story: "In 2019, my doctor told me something about belly fat that no one had mentioned in 20 years of trying."
  C) Fear:  "If you're over 45 and your belly fat isn't moving — there's a specific reason. And it's not what you think."

[PROBLEM STORY — 30s-2min]
- Set the scene: specific avatar, specific moment of pain
- Make the before-state visceral and specific
- "She'd tried everything. Keto. Fasting. Weighing every meal."
- The emotional low point: the dressing room, the doctor's scale, the family photo

[FAILED ATTEMPTS — 2-4min]
- Name every category they've tried
- Validate: "It's not your fault. The advice was wrong."
- Build: each failed attempt raises the stakes
- The false belief exposed: "Calories in, calories out stops working after 45"

[THE DISCOVERY — 4-6min]
- The inciting incident: how the mechanism was found
- Should feel like a story, not a lecture
- One clear villain (cortisol, inflammation, insulin — pick one)
- "The moment I understood this — everything made sense"

[MECHANISM EXPLAINED — 6-8min]
- Name the mechanism clearly (e.g., "The Cortisol Loop")
- Explain in 5th grade language — no jargon
- Why nothing else has worked against it
- Why this specific approach targets it directly

[PROOF STACK — 8-9min]
- 3-5 customer results: name, age, specific result, timeframe
- Clinical backing if applicable (ingredient studies, not product claims)
- Before/after images or video testimonials
- Guarantee framing: risk is on us, not on them

[OFFER + CTA — 9-10min]
- Name what they get (product + bonuses)
- Stack the value: "If you bought each piece separately..."
- Price reveal: anchor high, reveal actual price
- Guarantee: repeat it. Make it feel safe.
- Scarcity if real: inventory, production, date-limited bonus
- Final CTA: specific action, what happens next

[CLOSING HOOK — final 30s]
Restate the transformation in one sentence.
"You've tried doing it harder. This is doing it differently."
```

## UGC-Style Script (Any Platform — 15-60s)

Looks like organic content from a real customer. No logo, no polish, no brand feel.

```
[0-3s — HOOK — casual, unpolished]
"Okay I have to share this because I genuinely can't believe it worked."

[3-15s — PROBLEM — personal, specific]
"I've struggled with [problem] for [timeframe]. I tried [X], [Y], [Z]."

"Nothing. Worked."

[15-35s — DISCOVERY + RESULT]
"Then a friend told me about [product/mechanism]."

"I was skeptical. Honestly I almost didn't try it."

"But [specific result] in [timeframe] later — I'm a convert."

[35-50s — SPECIFIC PROOF]
"Like, my [specific metric] went from [before] to [after]."

"I'm not exaggerating. Here's what it looked like."

[VISUAL: Real result, photo, or measurement]

[50-60s — CTA — soft, not salesy]
"Anyway. I'll leave the link in my bio if you're curious."

"Zero pressure. Just wanted to share."
```
