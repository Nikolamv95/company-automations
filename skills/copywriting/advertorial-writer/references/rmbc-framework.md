# RMBC Framework — Advertorial Structure Reference

## What RMBC Stands For

```
R = Reason Why    (why is this information being shared — not to sell, to help)
M = Mechanism     (the unique thing that makes this work — UMP + UMS)
B = Benefits      (specific outcomes the mechanism delivers)
C = Call to Action (specific, low-friction next step)
```

---

## Template 11a — Nightmare (Pain/Transformation)

**Use when:** Angle leads with pain, before/after, transformation, "I used to..."

**Total length:** 900-1,200 words

---

### Section 1 — Headline + Deck
*(not in word count)*

- Headline: curiosity or fear hook, 10-20 words
- Deck: expand the promise, 1-2 sentences
- Product name does NOT appear here

---

### Section 2 — The Before (150-200 words)

Close 3rd person or 1st person. Make the reader say "That's me."

- Specific moment when the problem hits hardest (sensory, visceral)
- Not vague: NOT "struggling with weight"
- YES: "Pulling her jeans over her hips every morning, feeling the denim dig in, wondering when she stopped recognizing herself in the mirror"

---

### Section 3 — Failed Attempts (150-200 words)

Validate why they haven't solved this yet.

- 2-3 things they've already tried (from avatar research — use their exact language)
- For each: acknowledge it makes sense to try, then explain specifically why it fails
- End with: "It's not your fault. You were never told the real reason [problem persists]."

---

### Section 4 — The Discovery (200-250 words)

How was the solution found? Story + credibility through specificity.

- Who found it: researcher, doctor, person like the avatar
- The aha moment — what changed their understanding
- Specific: names, places, dates where credible
- The UMP (Unique Mechanism of Problem) is revealed here — the real hidden cause
- Must be counterintuitive but immediately make sense

---

### Section 5 — The Mechanism (150-200 words)

What makes this product different at a mechanistic level?

- Name the mechanism clearly (the UMS — Unique Mechanism of Solution)
- Explain why other approaches miss the real cause
- How the UMS directly addresses the UMP
- **Product name appears here for the first time**
- 5th grade language throughout

---

### Section 6 — The After (150-200 words)

Show the transformation. Specific, visual, emotional.

- Life after the mechanism works
- Real testimonial or composite story
- Mirror the sensory details from Section 2 (before/after contrast)
- Specific numbers: timeframes, measurements, results

---

### Section 7 — Proof (100-150 words)

2-3 proof points integrated naturally:

- Testimonial with specific result (not vague: "lost 23 lbs in 6 weeks" not "great results")
- Data point or research reference with source
- Guarantee or risk reversal

---

### Section 8 — Offer + CTA (100-150 words)

- What they get (core product + bonuses if any)
- Price
- Guarantee (specific, not vague — "60-day money-back" not "satisfaction guaranteed")
- CTA — single, simple, low friction: "Click here to check availability"

---

## Template 11c — Listicle (Curiosity-Led)

**Use when:** Angle is curiosity-led, "X reasons why," "X mistakes," shareable content

**Best for:** Facebook native, Taboola, Outbrain

**Structure:**
1. Opening — bold claim or curiosity hook
2. List items — 5-9 items, each with a mini-revelation
3. Mechanism reveal — buried in item 3-4 (product as the solution)
4. Proof — woven throughout
5. Offer + CTA at end

---

## Heath Wilcock PAS Flow (applies to all templates)

- **Pain:** Identify and agitate the core problem
- **Agitate:** Make the problem feel urgent and costly
- **Solution:** Present the discovery and results

---

## Subheadline Strategy

Every 150-200 words. Subheadlines should work as standalone story beats:
- State facts, not questions
- Include specific outcomes or numbers where possible
- Build progressive revelation — each one creates urgency to read more
- Format: `## [Specific Outcome/Revelation]`
