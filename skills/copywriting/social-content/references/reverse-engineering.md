# Reverse Engineering Viral Content

Instead of guessing what works, systematically analyze top-performing content in your niche and extract proven patterns.

Adapted from platform-agnostic best practices for DR marketing brands.

---

## The 6-Step Framework

### 1. NICHE ID — Find Top Creators

Identify 10-20 accounts in your space that consistently get high engagement:

**Selection criteria:**
- Posting consistently (3+ times/week)
- High engagement rate relative to follower count
- Audience overlap with your target avatar
- Mix of brands, affiliates, and organic creators (not just competitors)

**Where to find them:**
- Facebook: Search by problem keywords ("belly fat after 40", "thinning hair women")
- Instagram: Explore tab for your niche hashtags
- TikTok: Search by symptom/desire, sort by "Most Liked"
- YouTube: Search the problem, filter by most viewed (past year)
- Look for: affiliates of competing brands, niche influencers, health coaches

---

### 2. SCRAPE — Collect Posts at Scale

Gather 100-500+ posts from identified accounts for analysis:

**Tools:**
- **Apify** — Facebook, Instagram, TikTok scrapers
- **Phantom Buster** — Multi-platform automation
- **Manual collection** — Copy top posts into a spreadsheet for smaller datasets

**Data to collect:**
- Post text / video script (or transcript)
- Engagement metrics (likes, comments, shares, saves, views)
- Post format (text, image, carousel, video/Reel)
- Posting time/day
- Hook / first line / first 2 seconds
- CTA used
- Topic / angle (problem, mechanism, testimonial, myth-bust, etc.)

---

### 3. ANALYZE — Extract What Actually Works

Sort by engagement rate, identify the top 10-15% of posts.

**Quantitative:**
- Which format outperforms? (Video vs. image vs. carousel)
- Which day/time gets most engagement?
- What's the average length of top posts vs. average posts?
- Do videos under 30 seconds beat longer ones?

**Qualitative (for top performers):**
- What emotion does the hook trigger? (fear, curiosity, desire, validation)
- Is the hook about the problem or the result?
- Is there a specific number in the hook?
- What's the CTA? (Comment, link, follow, save)
- What topic/angle repeats in the top 15%?

**Questions to answer:**
- What hook patterns appear in top 10%?
- Which angles (problem, mechanism, testimonial, myth) perform best?
- What CTAs drive the most comments?
- What topics get saved/shared most?

---

### 4. PLAYBOOK — Codify the Patterns

Document repeatable patterns:

**Hook patterns:**
```
Pattern: "She [tried everything] for [time]. Then [discovery]."
Type: Story / transformation
Emotion: Validation + curiosity
Why it works: Avatar recognition + open loop

Pattern: "The real reason [problem] won't go away isn't [assumed cause]."
Type: Myth-bust / mechanism
Emotion: Curiosity + relief
Why it works: Challenges belief + promises revelation

Pattern: "[Number] [symptoms] that mean [problem] is actually [real cause]"
Type: List / diagnostic
Emotion: Fear + recognition
Why it works: Self-identification + credibility

Pattern: "[Common advice] is making your [problem] worse."
Type: Contrarian / fear
Emotion: Fear + pattern interrupt
Why it works: Stops scroll + high stakes
```

**Format patterns:**
- Carousel: Hook slide → Problem validation → Solution/mechanism → Proof → CTA
- Story post: Hook → Setup → Discovery → Result → Bridge to reader
- Reel: Spoken hook (1-2s) → Problem (5s) → Mechanism (15s) → Result/proof (5s) → CTA (3s)

**CTA patterns:**
- Question: "Have you experienced this?" (drives comments)
- Save signal: "Save this if you've been struggling with X"
- Share: "Tag someone who needs to hear this"
- Bridge: "Full story / link in comments"

---

### 5. LAYER VOICE — Apply DR Principles

Take proven patterns and layer direct-response copy principles on top:

**Specificity beats vagueness:**
```
❌ "She lost a lot of weight"
✅ "She lost 23 lbs in 6 weeks"

❌ "After struggling for years"
✅ "After 4 years of trying every diet"

❌ "Many women have seen results"
✅ "2,847 women have used this since January"
```

**Avatar language, not marketing language:**
```
❌ "Optimize your metabolic function"
✅ "Stop feeling exhausted by 2pm every day"

❌ "Support healthy cortisol levels"
✅ "Finally sleep through the night without waking at 3am"
```

**Emotion before information:**
```
❌ "Cortisol affects fat storage in postmenopausal women"
✅ "I was terrified when my doctor said my belly fat had nothing to do with calories."
```

**Short. Breathe. Land. (Especially for Reels/TikTok):**
```
❌ "After years of trying different approaches to address my stubborn belly fat, I finally
    discovered that the real problem wasn't my diet or exercise routine."

✅ "I tried everything.
   Keto. Fasting. Daily walks.
   Nothing moved.

   Then I found out why."
```

---

### 6. CONVERT — Bridge Organic to Purchase

**Soft conversions (build the list):**
- "Comment X and I'll send you the full breakdown" (DM automation)
- "Link in bio for the free guide" (lead magnet)
- "Save this and check back — we post new results weekly"

**Direct conversions (warm audience):**
- "Full story + what she used → link in comments"
- Product mention after educational value: "This is the mechanism [Product] targets"
- Retargeting pixel fires on page visits — organic traffic feeds paid retargeting

**Key principle:** Organic social = top-of-funnel awareness + warm retargeting pool. Don't try to close cold audiences organically. Let ads do the conversion work.

---

## The Formula

```
1. Find what's already working for similar audiences (don't guess)
2. Extract hook patterns, format patterns, CTA patterns
3. Layer DR copy principles (specificity, avatar language, emotion-first)
4. Test and iterate based on your own engagement data
```
