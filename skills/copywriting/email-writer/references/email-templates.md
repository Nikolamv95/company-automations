# Email Templates

Ready-to-use templates for individual DR emails. Each follows: Opening Hook → Story Body → Bridge → CTA → P.S.

---

## Template 1 — The Transformation Story Email

Use for: Welcome sequence Email 1, testimonial emails, social proof emails.

```
Subject:    she wore a swimsuit for the first time in 3 years
Preview:    Here's what changed for her (and what might change for you)

[Opening hook — pulls them into someone else's story immediately]
Linda hadn't worn a swimsuit in three years.

Not because she didn't want to.

Because every time she tried on a suit in the dressing room, she'd leave without buying it.

[Story body — situation → problem → discovery. Short paragraphs.]
She'd done everything "right."

Low carb. Daily walks. Cutting sugar.

The scale barely moved.

What nobody told her was this:

After 45, the rules change. Your body doesn't respond to the same inputs anymore.

Linda found out why. And it had nothing to do with willpower.

[Bridge — connects her story to the mechanism or product]
The reason most women struggle after menopause isn't discipline.

It's cortisol. And most diets make it worse.

[CTA — single, specific action]
I wrote up exactly what Linda did here:

→ [Her full story + what worked]

[P.S.]
P.S. Linda lost 19 lbs in 8 weeks. She wore a bikini on her 54th birthday. If you're dealing with the same frustration, her story might change things for you.
```

## Hook Formulas (Opening Lines)

The first 1-2 sentences determine whether the reader continues. Do not start with "I hope this email finds you well."

```
Story hook:     "[Name] hadn't [desired action] in [timeframe]."
Problem hook:   "If you've been [struggle], you're not imagining it."
Curiosity hook: "Something strange happened when [unexpected event]."
Result hook:    "[Number/result] in [timeframe]. No [common sacrifice]."
Contrarian:     "Everything you've been told about [problem] is wrong."
Question hook:  "What would you do if [desired outcome] happened in [timeframe]?"
```

## Story Arc Structure

For longer story emails (200-400 words), follow this arc:

```
1. SITUATION   — Who is this about? Set the scene in 1-2 sentences.
2. STRUGGLE    — What have they tried? Validate the frustration.
3. INCIDENT    — What specific moment or discovery changed things?
4. DISCOVERY   — What did they find out? (mechanism or insight)
5. RESULT      — Specific, with numbers and timeframe.
6. BRIDGE      — "If you're dealing with the same thing..."
7. CTA         — One clear action.
8. P.S.        — Reinforce desire or urgency.
```
