# Personal Discovery Story — Structure Reference

The primary format for long-form desire-testing and angle-testing ad copy (Steps 8 and 10). 1200-1400 words.

**The story premise:** Someone in crisis discovers the REAL reason behind their problem — one nobody talks about — and finds hope. This isn't a company selling. It's someone sharing a desperate discovery that saved something precious.

---

## Story Arc

### 1. Opening Hook (max 20 words)
An irresistible first line that makes them NEED to know what happens next. Use a specific moment: a conversation, an observation, a crisis beat.

> "Max was dying, and it was my fault."
> "My doctor GASPED when she saw my results."

### 2. Vulnerability — Why This Matters
- Show emotional stakes through specific daily moments
- Make the reader recognize themselves: "that's exactly my situation"
- Build to a breaking point or crisis moment
- Use sensory details — times, places, physical feelings

### 3. The Desperate Search
- Late-night googling, useless advice, timestamps ("3am again...")
- Failed attempts with common solutions and WHY they failed
- Show increasing desperation — the stakes are rising

### 4. The Discovery (THE MOST IMPORTANT SECTION)
The real reason behind the problem — counterintuitive, nobody talks about it.

**Choose what fits the story — do NOT default to Reddit/forum posts:**
- A doctor/expert who finally explains what's really happening
- Your own observation that suddenly makes everything click
- A book/study that contradicts everything you believed
- Someone else's casual comment that changes your perspective
- A test result that reveals the hidden issue
- Trying something different out of desperation and noticing unexpected results

**Rule:** The unique mechanism (UMP + UMS) emerges through YOUR realization, not through quoted conversations. No structured reddit-style quote blocks.

### 5. The Transformation
- Skepticism → hope → small changes → real result
- Specific sensory details of the change
- Concrete before/after (numbers, timeframes, moments)

### 6. Circle Back
- Return to the emotional stakes from the opening
- What would have been lost without this discovery
- What this means in time, moments, relationships, experiences

### 7. Soft Close
- End with soft urgency: sharing crucial information, not selling
- The product name should appear here (after discovery moment)
- Single clear CTA
- No hard sell — they feel like you're doing them a favor

---

## What Makes the Discovery Credible

The UMP (Unique Mechanism of Problem) must be:
- Counterintuitive but immediately make sense
- Explain WHY all previous solutions failed
- Create an "aha moment" — everything suddenly clicks

The UMS (Unique Mechanism of Solution) must:
- Directly address the UMP
- Explain HOW it solves the real problem (not just what it does)
- Make confidence: "of course that would work"

---

## Writing Style

- Short paragraphs — 1-3 sentences max
- Natural line breaks
- Specific details: times, names, numbers, places
- Physical reactions: what they felt in their body
- Conversational, first-person, present-tense for drama
- NO headers or section dividers inside the story
- NO structured quote blocks from forums

---

## Step 8 vs Step 10 Difference

**Step 8 (Desire Testing):** One ad. Tests the PRIMARY DESIRE. Does this desire resonate?
- Different copies test different desires

**Step 10 (Angle Testing):** Three ads. Tests different ANGLES around the SAME desire.
- Each variant opens with a different angle's hook
- The desire is constant — the entry point changes
- Label each variant with which angle it tests
