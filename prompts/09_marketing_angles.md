---

## What Is an Angle

An angle is the STORY that activates someone's desire. Each angle must tell a different type of story that resonates with a different segment of people who share the same core desire.

---

## Critical Requirements

- Each angle must feature a DIFFERENT "moment of realization" or crisis point
- Each must appeal to people in different life circumstances
- Each must reveal the problem through a unique lens
- Use actual patterns from the forum/avatar research — real situations, real language

---

## Structure for Each Angle

```
Angle Name: [Descriptive title]
Target Audience: [Who specifically relates to this story]
Story Arc: [Beginning situation → Crisis/realization moment → Discovery → Resolution]
Unique Problem Mechanism: [What specifically causes the problem in THIS story]
Unique Solution Mechanism: [How the solution works differently in THIS context]
Key Emotional Moment: [The specific "rock bottom" or turning point]
```

---

## Test for Distinction

Could these be 5 different people at a support group meeting, each sharing a COMPLETELY DIFFERENT story about how they discovered the same problem?

If two stories feel similar, they're not distinct enough.

---

## Example Format

**Angle: "The 3 AM Emergency"**
- Audience: Pet parents who've experienced health crises
- Story Arc: Normal evening → pet crisis → emergency vet → discovering preventable cause
- Problem Mechanism: Chronic dehydration from water bowl aversion
- Solution Mechanism: Fountain prevents biofilm that triggers avoidance
- Emotional Moment: "What if we're too late?"

---

## Instructions

Draw heavily from the actual language and situations in the forum research from `03_avatar.md` and `06_desire_validation.md`. We want real experiences, not generic scenarios.

Write all 5 angles. End with a recommendation for which angle to test first and why (1-2 sentences).
