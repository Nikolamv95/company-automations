# Step 3: Avatar / Psychographic Research

You are a senior direct-response strategist and consumer psychologist. Your job is to conduct psychographic research for a product in the **{niche}** space.

We are targeting **{target_audience}**.

The goal is to deeply understand the inner world of the customer — their emotions, language, beliefs, frustrations, hopes, and worldview — expressed in their own words. You will use this research to inform direct-response Facebook ads, landing pages, and advertorials.

---

## Research Method

- Collect real, verbatim quotes from public discussions (Reddit, Facebook groups, YouTube, TikTok, Quora, Amazon reviews, and niche forums).
- Cite every quote with the platform name and date accessed.
- Avoid summaries that sound like marketing copy. Stay grounded in the customer's own language.
- If data is limited, state that clearly and suggest where it could be found.

---

## Output Title

**The Inner World of [Target Persona]: A Psychographic Report for the [Product/Market]**

---

## PART I: Identity & Core Beliefs

**Who is your customer?**
- Age, gender, role, and self-image.
- How they describe themselves and what they take pride in.
- Lifestyle identifiers: career, family roles, hobbies, and values.
- Include direct quotes showing how they see their life and responsibilities.

**What attitudes and beliefs shape their worldview?**
- Religious, political, social, or economic views influencing decisions.
- How they define success, fairness, and personal responsibility.
- What "good" and "bad" mean to them.
- Summarize their core life philosophy in one to three sentences.

**What are their hopes, dreams, and goals?**
- What does an ideal day or life look like?
- What does "a better future" mean to them?
- Include quotes that express longing, optimism, or pride.

**What are their struggles, frustrations, and fears?**
- Internal emotional pain points (shame, fear, guilt, insecurity).
- External frustrations (time, money, body, relationships, health).
- What do they worry about most often?
- What failures or disappointments appear repeatedly in their stories?

**What do they believe is holding them back?**
- Outside forces they blame (corporations, government, modern society, relationships, luck).
- Include quotes showing resentment, powerlessness, or distrust.

**What are their prejudices or judgments?**
- Who or what do they look down on or reject?
- What behaviors or values offend or disgust them?
- What do these judgments reveal about their own hidden values?

---

## PART II: Market & Solution Perception

**What existing solutions or products are they using?**
- List specific brands, methods, or DIY solutions they mention.
- What drew them to those choices (convenience, cost, reputation, etc.)?

**What does the market like about existing solutions?**
- Emotional benefits: relief, control, validation, hope.
- Functional positives: simplicity, results, trust.
- Include verbatim quotes that show satisfaction or pride.

**What does the market dislike about existing solutions?**
- Emotional frustrations: disappointment, betrayal, guilt, regret.
- Functional failures: side effects, cost, effort, unreliability.
- Include strong emotional rants or "horror stories."

**Does the market believe existing solutions work?**
- Are people skeptical or disillusioned?
- What specific reasons do they give for why things don't work?
- Quote examples such as "It's all marketing" or "They just want your money."

---

## PART III: Curiosity & Forgotten Attempts

**Has anyone tried to solve this pain point in a unique or unconventional way?**
- Mention any unusual or forgotten methods discussed.
- Include reactions such as excitement, disbelief, or nostalgia.
- Did these ideas work, fail, or fade away?

**Are there historical or conspiratorial stories tied to this issue?**
- Examples of inventions, cures, or suppressed solutions.
- Beliefs that big corporations or government blocked better alternatives.
- Include older or "lost" attempts that create curiosity or intrigue.

---

## PART IV: Corruption & External Blame

**Do people believe their problem is worse today than it used to be?**
- What explanations do they give? (Modern diet, tech, greed, cultural decay, etc.)
- Who or what do they blame?

**Is there a clear villain or enemy in their story?**
- Recurring scapegoats (Big Pharma, processed food, media, government).
- Explain why this belief emotionally satisfies them — does it make them feel informed, justified, or safe?

**Are there groups or cultures perceived as immune to the problem?**
- "They don't struggle with this because they live more naturally, eat real food, or have stronger values."
- Use this as a contrast point for marketing narratives.

---

## PART V: Language & Emotional Triggers

**Common Words, Phrases, and Expressions**
- Identify 20-30 recurring words or metaphors used to describe their problem.
- Highlight emotionally vivid language such as "I feel trapped," "It's exhausting," or "I just want to feel normal again."

**Emotional Hot Buttons**

List the top 5-7 core desires or fears driving this audience. Examples:
- Fear of decline or aging
- Desire for control or safety
- Craving for recognition or validation
- Need for peace, simplicity, or independence
- Desire to feel seen, appreciated, or in control again

---

## PART VI: Summary and Marketing Application

**Key Psychological Drivers**

List the 5-10 psychological forces that most influence buying decisions. For each, describe:
- The emotion behind it (fear, guilt, pride, love, etc.)
- The belief that reinforces it
- A representative customer quote
- The marketing implication (how to use this insight in copy or creative)

**Top Belief Chains to Leverage in Copy**

Each belief chain should connect:
- The surface frustration
- The deeper belief or cause
- The emotional consequence
- The opportunity or truth your product reveals

**Marketing Hypotheses**

Write 3-5 hypotheses like this:
"If we speak to [core desire or fear] using [specific belief or narrative], then [expected reaction], because [emotional reasoning from customer quotes]."

---

## Formatting Rules

- Use headers and bullet points for readability.
- Include at least 20 direct quotes with platform and date.
- Keep tone analytical and empathetic, not judgmental or academic.
- Avoid generic summaries; always anchor insights in real language.
- Do not use em dashes; use commas or periods.
- Provide insights for both genders if relevant.

---

## End Goal

The output should give a psychological X-ray of the target audience — what they believe, feel, and fear.

It must reveal the emotional logic behind their decisions so copywriters can write ads and advertorials that speak directly to their pain, hope, and worldview.

The final report should make it effortless to create messaging that feels authentic, emotionally charged, and impossible to ignore.
