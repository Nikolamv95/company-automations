## Critical Framework: RMBC Structure (Non-Negotiable)

### Lead
- Call out problem with statistics/urgency
- Promise solution + tease discovery story
- Tease unique mechanism (contrarian/surprising)
- Brief credibility builders
- Qualify audience

### Background Story
- Customer narrator (NOT company owner)
- "I was just like you" connection
- Traditional solutions failing
- Trigger event/crisis moment
- Search for truth/answers

### Unique Mechanism of Problem (UMP)
- Surprising/counterintuitive explanation
- "99% know this, but the missing 1% is..."
- Scientific backing/credibility

### Unique Mechanism of Solution (UMS)
- Logical connection to problem
- Specific methodology/technology
- Why this works when others fail

### Product Buildup + Reveal
- Customer discovered product through research
- Testing/results story
- Proof it works
- Others asking for it

### Close
- Product details and differentiators
- Social proof/testimonials
- Urgency/scarcity
- Guarantee
- Price justification
- Multiple CTAs
- Two-choice close

## Heath Wilcock PAS Flow

- **Pain:** Identify and agitate the core problem
- **Agitate:** Make the problem feel urgent and costly
- **Solution:** Present the discovery and results

---

## Writing Style Requirements

**Sentences:**
- Keep most sentences under 15 words
- Use simple words (5th grade level)
- Break up complex ideas into multiple short sentences
- Vary sentence length for rhythm

**Paragraphs:**
- 1-3 sentences maximum
- Lots of white space
- Easy to scan

**Opening Hook:**
- Start with ONE short, punchy sentence that cuts to core pain/fear
- 6-12 words maximum
- Must create immediate emotional impact
- Example: "Max was dying, and it was my fault."

**Headlines throughout:**
- Create curiosity and promise benefits
- Avoid starting with "The"
- Example: "How A $3,200 Emergency Changed Everything"

**Bolding for scannability:**
- Key statistics and numbers
- Shocking revelations
- Solution benefits and results
- Urgency/scarcity elements
- Guarantee terms
- CTA phrases

## Customer Story Format

- Narrator is a CUSTOMER who discovered the product
