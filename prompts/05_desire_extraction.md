# Step 5: Mass Desire Extraction

I want you to analyze this product using Eugene Schwartz's framework of performances and mass desires.

Because I don't have the product with me, I am going to give you my competitor's product and an Amazon listing of the same/similar product I'll be selling.

**Competitor URL:** {competitor_urls}
**Product URL:** {product_url}

## Instructions (follow in this exact order)

**1.** List all **direct performances** — the physical and functional actions the product performs.

**2.** List all **secondary or consequential performances** — what happens as a result of those direct actions (e.g., spinal alignment → less snoring; better sleep → more energy; comfort → improved mood). Do not skip or overlook these secondary outcomes.

**3.** For each performance, map it to the underlying **human mass desire** it fulfills:
   - health
   - relief from pain
   - beauty
   - confidence
   - recognition
   - relationships
   - saving money
   - freedom
   - convenience
   - (etc.)

**4.** Score each mapped desire using Schwartz's **three dimensions**:
   - **Urgency:** Is this pain/need immediate and pressing, or low-priority?
   - **Staying Power:** Does it repeat daily/chronically, or is it temporary?
   - **Scope:** How many people share this desire (niche vs mass market)?

   Give each a 1-5 score with one sentence of reasoning.

**5.** From this evaluation, identify the **single dominant performance + desire** that has the most selling power (highest urgency + staying power + scope).

**6.** Show how all other performances can be used as **supporting proof or reinforcements** for that dominant performance.

**7.** Create **3 example headlines** based on the dominant performance:
   - One for a problem-aware market
   - One for a solution-aware market
   - One for a product-aware market

**8.** Take all of your desires and phrase them as **"I want..." statements** so we can view them from the customer's POV.

---

## Output Format

Structure the output clearly with numbered sections matching the steps above.

End with a ranked list of all desires as "I want..." statements, from strongest to weakest based on your scoring.
