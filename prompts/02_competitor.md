Your task is to conduct competitor research for a product in the **{niche}** space.

Your output should be highly structured, evidence-based, and include links and dates accessed for every factual claim.
If information is not available, mark it as N/A and suggest where it could be found.

---

## Research Objectives

For each competitor you find, I want to understand the following in clear, structured sections:

1. **Who is the target demographic they speak to?**
   - Include gender, age range, interests, income level, and psychographics.
   - Describe the tone, visuals, and personality of their marketing that indicates who they're trying to attract.

2. **What are their main new-customer acquisition funnels?**
   - List all primary traffic sources (Facebook, TikTok, Google, YouTube, influencers, affiliates, etc.).
   - Include example ads or campaigns and link to ad library listings if possible.
   - Describe the main funnel flow (Ad → LP or Advertorial → PDP → Cart → Checkout → Upsells).

3. **What is their core messaging in their ads, advertorials, and other advertising assets?**
   - Summarize their main selling ideas, emotional triggers, and key copy angles.
   - Include examples of headlines, hooks, or body copy snippets (with links).
   - Identify the awareness stage(s) each message targets (Unaware, Problem-aware, Solution-aware, Product-aware, Most-aware).

4. **Are there recurring or repetitive hooks, angles, or big ideas?**
   - List all common patterns or creative themes across their marketing.
   - Explain why each one likely resonates with their target audience.
   - Highlight the top 3 most frequently used hooks with direct quotes and links.

5. **Are there any major gaps or missed opportunities?**
   - Look for missing awareness levels, weak proof, untested creative angles, or poor differentiation.
   - Identify unique advantages we could capitalize on or areas where we could outperform them.

6. **What is their pricing structure and offer stack?**
   - Include product price points, bundles, subscription models, shipping costs, and guarantees.
   - Note bonuses, free gifts, scarcity tactics (limited stock, countdowns), and financing options.

7. **What do customers love and dislike about them?**
   - Use reviews, Reddit, TikTok comments, and social signals to summarize customer sentiment.
   - List top "love" themes (ranked by frequency) and top "hate" themes (ranked by frequency).
   - Include at least 10 positive and 10 negative verbatim quotes with links and sources.

8. **Who or what do they use for proof and authority?**
   - UGC or influencer content
   - Expert endorsements or partnerships
   - Press coverage or certifications
   - Testimonials, case studies, or before-after examples

9. **What disclaimers or compliance tactics are visible?**
   - Note any health, performance, or guarantee disclaimers they include on their sites or advertorials.
   - Highlight any missing disclosures that could pose compliance or FTC risks.

10. **If available, what is their estimated revenue?**
    - Include estimated company revenue (from tools like SimilarWeb, Growjo, or public filings).
    - Include estimated revenue or sales for their hero product(s) most similar to ours.
    - Cite every data source and date accessed.

---

## Output Structure

Organize the final document with clear section headers for each competitor, in this order:

1. Overview (brand, site, hero products, estimated revenue, platform type)
2. Target Demographic Summary
3. Funnel Breakdown
4. Messaging and Awareness Analysis
5. Hook and Angle Patterns
6. Offer and Pricing Breakdown
7. Proof and Credibility Assets
8. Review Mining (Love vs Hate)
9. Compliance Review
10. Gaps and Opportunities

After listing all competitors, include a **Market Summary** section that covers:
- Top 3 competitor strengths
- Top 3 weaknesses or missed opportunities
- Dominant awareness levels across the market
- 3-5 new angles or hypotheses we can test in ads or landing pages

---

## Formatting Rules

- Use bullet points and short paragraphs for clarity.
- Include URLs and date accessed for every cited claim or example.
- Keep tone factual and analytical, not speculative.
- Avoid em dashes; use commas or periods instead.
- Provide insights for at least five direct competitors, or explain why fewer are available.
- Use headings and subheadings for clarity.

---

## End Goal

The goal of this competitor research is to create a clear strategic foundation for writing direct-response sales letters, advertorials, and landing pages.

Your job is to surface every key insight, hook, offer, and opportunity that can be used to outperform current market leaders.
